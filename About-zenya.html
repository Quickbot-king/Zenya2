Meet Our Team

Hilda Mary Nabukonde

Advanced Diploma in Bible Study (Australia) | B.A. in Counselling Psychology (Tangaza University) | Master’s Degree (Ongoing).


Hilda Mary Nabukonde is a dedicated and compassionate counselling professional specializing in trauma therapy. With an Advanced Diploma in Bible Study from Australia and a Bachelor's degree in Counselling Psychology from Tangaza University, she brings a unique integration of faith-based and evidence-based approaches to psychological care.

Currently pursuing a Master's degree, Hilda has developed expertise in supporting adolescents and young adults through challenging life experiences, with a strong focus on trauma counselling. Her therapeutic approach emphasizes emotional healing, resilience-building, and holistic well-being, empowering clients to navigate complex psychological and spiritual challenges.

Hilda is passionate about creating safe, supportive spaces for young individuals to process trauma, rebuild self-worth, and develop positive coping strategies, fostering long-term mental and emotional health.

Dennis Murabula Obuya, MSc, BSc

Oncology Specialist | Clinical Psychologist | Onco-psychology Expert.


Dennis Murabula Obuya is a seasoned oncology professional with over 20 years of experience in cancer care. He holds a Bachelor’s degree in Radiotherapy and a Master’s degree in Clinical Psychology, enabling him to offer a rare blend of medical expertise and psychological support to patients and families navigating the challenges of cancer.

Specializing in onco-psychology and counselling, Dennis is passionate about addressing the emotional, psychological, and social needs of individuals affected by cancer. His holistic approach integrates compassionate counselling with evidence-based therapeutic interventions, empowering patients to build resilience, cope effectively, and enhance their overall well-being throughout treatment and survivorship.

Driven by a deep commitment to patient advocacy and mental health awareness in oncology, Dennis works closely with patients, caregivers, and multidisciplinary teams to ensure that cancer care goes beyond medical treatment—fostering hope, emotional healing, and improved quality of life.
